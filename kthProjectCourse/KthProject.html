Project Overview

Built for motion

Airchestra makes it possible to play instruments and create music without owning a single physical instrument. A webcam observes your movement, the software interprets your gestures, and the system translates that motion into musical actions.

Instruments

Choose your sound

Airchestra turns familiar instruments into gesture-controlled modes, giving each performer a different way to shape melody, rhythm, and expression through movement.

Theremin

Play hovering tones with smooth hand movement, shaping pitch and expression without touching anything.

Guitar

Strum and accent chords with broad motions, bringing a more physical performance style into the mix.

Keyboard

Trigger clear notes and simple melodies through mapped gestures that feel precise and easy to learn.

Drums

Build beats from punchy gestures, using body movement to keep time and layer rhythmic hits.

Music without walls

The core idea is to make music more accessible, playful, and immediate. Many instruments are expensive, hard to store, or intimidating at first. Airchestra removes that barrier by letting anyone experiment with instrumental sound using only a camera, a screen, and their own movement.
