WORK IN PROGRESS

Project Overview

Airchestra makes it possible to play instruments and create music without owning a single physical instrument. Instead of needing a violin, a drum kit, or a keyboard in front of you, the project uses software to turn your body into the interface. A webcam observes your movement and passes that motion into the system. The software then interprets your gestures and translates them into musical actions. In this way, anyone can begin experimenting with instrumental sound using only a camera, a screen, and their own movement.

Concept

The core idea is to make music feel more accessible, playful, and immediate. Many instruments can be expensive, difficult to store, or intimidating to learn at first. Airchestra removes that barrier by letting the user choose the instrument they want to play inside the software. Once an instrument is selected, the webcam tracks the user's motion and maps it to sounds associated with that instrument. The result is a digital performance space where movement becomes music.

Experience

Using Airchestra should feel like stepping into an invisible orchestra. The player can move naturally in front of the webcam and hear their gestures transformed into sound in real time. Different movements can be connected to different notes, rhythms, or expressive qualities depending on the selected instrument. This creates a way to explore music through motion rather than through traditional hardware. It opens up a space where performance, technology, and creativity meet in a simple and approachable form.
